Adults and Coffee Cups

Not quite proud of the alliteration in the title, but I could not help myself.

Drinking a cup of coffee is not a new thing, considering I used to get some whenever mom made extra. It used to feel like a “not me because I am a literal child” thing to do, to be handed a cup of hand-and-mouth-burning-if-you-lack-skill coffee in a delicate and fancy looking china or the ultimate level: stainless steel glass (shoutout to the hotels in Madras who will fill it above and beyond the brim and make it 10x harder to even touch the glass, let alone lift it) and be expected to enjoy it, let alone drink it without many things going wrong. Because it was for adults, it still is. The realization hit me (like the smell of coffee, gradually getting to intense) when I finished drinking a cup of coffee, felt satisfied, and thought to myself “well, that was a good cup of coffee” instead of “haha I am a kid, I get to pretend to be an adult and drink ‘for-adults-only’ coffee!”. The cup no longer felt out of place in my hand, specifically, my short finger(s) knew what they were supposed to do with it to aid a fairly comfortable consumption of the beverage.

Little did I remember at that very moment that it wasn’t, in fact, a mere coincidence. It was years of training kicking in… (who am I flattering, it was months at max)

I got used to drinking hot coffee from tiny stainless steel glasses, after walking with freshly-filled-with-coffee ones, bringing them from the counter to my table while at least 2-3 people almost bump into me. My hands had learnt how to do that, learning from every time I almost spilled it, had to speed-walk or do a pit stop because the moving hot liquid was unmanageable. Although I don’t crave the beverage that puts me right to sleep or induces a drowsy state like no other, I do appreciate the olfactory familiarity whenever I get to experience wherever I find it. The taste is still horrible, (sugar makes everything better!) but I can look (or taste) past that. I am probably a forgets-she-has-a-subscription member of the coffee drinking club, and now that I realise it, I have become a will-forever-be-new-here member of the adults club.
